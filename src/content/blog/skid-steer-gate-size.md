## The short answer

For most fenced backyards, a 36-inch compact skid steer is the only thing that fits — so measure the gate first, then book.

The single most common reason a backyard rental goes sideways is simple: the machine won't fit through the gate. Before you book a skid steer, the first question isn't power or attachments — it's **access**.

## The 36-inch rule

A compact, small-frame skid steer is roughly **36 inches wide**. That's narrow enough to pass through a standard 36-inch residential gate with the gate fully open. It's the machine that gets into fenced backyards, side yards, and tight courtyards.

Standard-frame skid steers run about **60 to 66 inches wide**. They have far more lift and breakout force — great on open lots and bigger grading jobs — but they will not fit through most residential gates. Don't assume; measure.

## How to measure your gate

- Measure the **actual clear opening**, post to post, with the gate fully swung open — not the gate panel width.
- Check for **posts, latches, and hinges** that eat into the opening.
- Look at the **approach**: can the machine line up straight, or is there a tight turn before the gate? A turn needs extra room.
- Mind **overhead** clearance — low branches and eaves matter too.

## Tracks or wheels?

Once it fits, the next call is tracks versus wheels. Tracked machines float on soft, wet, or turf ground and leave less of a mess — worth it in a soft Front Range spring. Wheels are faster and lower-cost on hard, established surfaces.

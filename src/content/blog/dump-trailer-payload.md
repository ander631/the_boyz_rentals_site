## The short answer

Don't load to the size of the box — load to payload (GVWR minus empty weight), and remember dense material hits that limit fast.

When people ask "how much can this dump trailer hold," they're usually thinking about the size of the box. The number that actually matters is **payload** — and it's smaller than most people expect.

## Payload = GVWR − empty weight

Every trailer has a **GVWR** (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating): the most the loaded trailer is rated to weigh. Subtract the trailer's **empty weight** and what's left is your payload — the weight you can legally carry.

A common **7x14 dump trailer** might be rated at **14,000 lb GVWR** and weigh around **4,400 lb empty**. That leaves roughly **9,000 to 9,600 lb** of payload. That's the figure you load to — not the length of the box.

## Dense material fills payload before volume

Here's the part that catches people out: heavy material hits the weight limit long before the box looks full.

- **Wet dirt, gravel, sand, concrete** — heavy. A half-full box can already be at the weight limit.
- **Mulch, brush, light debris** — bulky but light. You'll fill the box before you hit the weight.

Know which one you're hauling. Topping off a box of wet fill is how you end up overweight and unsafe.
